# Directive: Greek Easy Language (Εύκολη Ανάγνωση)

## Basis [M]
**Type:** Accessibility Standard
**Framework:** EU Accessibility Guidelines adapted for Greek
**Origin:** Greece (EU member state)

---

## Purpose [M]
Defines rules for translating texts into Greek Easy Language while maintaining legal accuracy (Hybrid Approach).
**Priority:** Legal correctness over maximum simplification. [M]

## Key Requirements [M]

### The Hybrid Rule [M]
Because texts may have legal relevance, **technical terms are preserved** and explained.

#### 1. Glossary Priority [M]
If the glossary contains an explanation: **You MUST use this explanation word-for-word.**

#### 2. The Anchor Phrase [M]
Use **exclusively** the Greek phrase: **"Αυτό σημαίνει:"** (This means).

#### 3. Anti-Doubling [M]
If the original sentence contains words like "σημαίνει:", "είναι:", "δηλαδή:" -> **DELETE these words** before adding "Αυτό σημαίνει:".

#### 4. Hard Line Break (Mandatory) [M]
Format:
1. Technical term (**bold**) + full stop.
2. New paragraph -> "Αυτό σημαίνει:" (alone).
3. New paragraph -> Explanation.

**Correct Example:**
> **Αποτελεσματικότητα**.
>
> Αυτό σημαίνει:
>
> Πόσα μέσα χρειάζονται για να επιτευχθεί ένας στόχος.

### Grammar & Sentence Structure [M]
- **One sentence = One idea** [M]
- **SVO order** (Subject - Verb - Object) [M]
- **Short sentences:** Maximum 12 words per sentence [M]
- **Active voice only** [M]
- **Avoid subjunctive mood:** Use indicative where possible [M]
- **Simple verb tenses:** Prefer present tense [M]

### Greek-Specific Rules [M]
- **Avoid diminutives** unless necessary for clarity
- **Use simple articles:** Prefer definite article (ο, η, το) for clarity
- **Compound words:** Split long compounds where possible (e.g., "αυτο-κίνητο" instead of "αυτοκίνητο" for emphasis)
- **Genitive case:** Simplify genitive constructions using "από" (from) or "του/της" clearly
- **Accent marks:** Always include proper polytonic/monotonic accents

### Vocabulary [M]
- Use everyday words
- Explain foreign loan words
- Avoid bureaucratic language
- Replace abstract concepts with concrete examples
