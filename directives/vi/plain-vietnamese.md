# Directive: Plain Vietnamese (Clear Communication)

## Basis [M]
**Type:** Clear Communication Standard
**Framework:** Plain Language Principles adapted for Vietnamese
**Origin:** Vietnam / International Plain Language Guidelines

---

## Purpose [M]
Defines rules for translating texts into Plain Vietnamese for general audiences seeking clear communication.

**Priority:** Clarity and readability while maintaining professional tone. [M]
**Inspiration:** General international Plain Language principles, adapted for Vietnamese.

## When to Use [M]
- For general audience texts requiring clarity
- For official communications (letters, announcements)
- For instructional materials
- When readability is important but full simplification is not required

---

## Key Requirements [M]

### The Hybrid Rule (Maintained) [M]
Technical terms are preserved and explained when necessary.

#### 1. Glossary Priority [M]
If the glossary contains an explanation:
**Use this explanation.**

#### 2. The "Nghĩa là" Anchor [M]
Use "Nghĩa là:" for inline explanations when needed.

**Format (Inline allowed):**
> **Hiệu quả** (Nghĩa là: mức độ đạt được mục tiêu với nguồn lực tối thiểu).

### Grammar & Sentence Structure [M]
- **One main idea per sentence** [M]
- **SVO order preferred** (Subject - Verb - Object) [M]
- **Active voice preferred** [M]
- Passive voice acceptable when appropriate [C]

### Vietnamese-Specific Rules [M]

#### Tonal Diacritics [M]
- **ALWAYS preserve diacritics correctly** [M]
- All tone marks must be accurate

#### Sentence Length [M]
- Maximum 20 words per sentence [M]
- Subordinate clauses allowed but keep them short [C]

#### Vocabulary [M]
- Sino-Vietnamese terms acceptable for educated audiences
- Explain only highly technical terms
- Balance between accessibility and appropriate register

### Formatting [M]
- Use paragraphs logically [M]
- Use headings for structure [C]
- Bullet points for lists [C]

---

## Edge Cases & Special Rules [M]

### A. Technical Vocabulary [M]
- Keep domain-specific terms when audience expects them
- Explain only when term is uncommon

### B. Abbreviations [M]
- Common abbreviations acceptable (TP. HCM, VN)
- Uncommon abbreviations should be expanded on first use

### C. Pronouns [M]
- Use appropriate pronouns for context
- "Quý khách" for formal customer communication
- "Bạn" for friendly, neutral tone
- "Anh/Chị" acceptable in semi-formal contexts

### D. Numbers and Dates [M]
- Numbers as digits for data
- Dates: "01/04/2024" or "ngày 1 tháng 4 năm 2024"

### E. Tone [M]
- Professional and respectful
- Avoid overly casual language
- Avoid overly bureaucratic language
