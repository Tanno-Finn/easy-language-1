# Directive: Sade Turkce (Plain Language)

## Basis [M]
**Type:** Plain Language Standard
**Framework:** General Plain Language principles adapted for Turkish
**Origin:** Adapted for Turkish language structure

---

## Purpose [M]
Defines rules for translating texts into Plain Turkish - clear, accessible language for general audiences. Less strict than Easy Read, but still prioritizing clarity.

**Priority:** Clarity and readability while maintaining professional tone. [M]
**Target Audience:** General public, busy professionals, non-native speakers.

## When to Use [M]
- For government and public communications [M]
- For business documents and reports [C]
- For websites and digital content [C]
- When broad accessibility is needed without cognitive disability focus

---

## Key Requirements [M]

### The Hybrid Rule (Adapted) [M]
Technical terms may be used but should be explained on first occurrence.

#### 1. Explanation Anchor [M]
Use "Yani:" or "Bu demek ki:" for inline explanations.
Unlike Easy Read, explanations can be inline (same paragraph).

**Format:**
> **Verimlilik**, yani bir hedefe ulasmak icin harcanan kaynak miktari, onemlidir.

#### 2. Glossary Option [C]
For documents with many technical terms, include a glossary at the end instead of inline explanations.

---

## Grammar Rules [M]

### A. Sentence Structure [M]
- **One main idea per sentence** [M]
- **Maximum 20-25 words per sentence** [C]
- **Prefer active voice** [M]
- Complex sentences allowed if structure is clear [C]

### B. Word Order [M]
- Follow natural Turkish SOV order [M]
- Keep subject and verb close when possible [C]
- Avoid overly nested clauses [M]

### C. Agglutination (Turkish-Specific) [M]
- Moderate suffix chains acceptable (up to 4-5 suffixes) [C]
- Break very long agglutinated forms into clearer structures [M]
- Maintain natural Turkish flow [M]

**Example:**
| Too Complex | Plain |
|-------------|-------|
| anlastirilamamis | uzlasma saglanamamis |
| gorusturulebileceklerinizden | gorusebileceginiz kisilerden |

### D. Voice [M]
- Prefer active voice [M]
- Passive acceptable when agent is unknown or unimportant [C]
- Avoid double passives [M]

---

## Word Choice [M]

### A. Vocabulary Level [M]
- Use common vocabulary understood by educated adults [M]
- Technical terms allowed with explanation on first use [M]
- Avoid unnecessary jargon [M]

### B. Formal vs. Informal [C]
- Use polite formal register (siz form) for official documents [C]
- Informal register (sen form) acceptable for casual content [C]
- Be consistent within a document [M]

### C. Loanwords [C]
- Widely-used loanwords acceptable (telefon, bilgisayar, internet)
- Prefer Turkish alternatives for less common foreign words [C]
- Avoid mixing languages unnecessarily [M]

**Examples:**
| Overly Foreign | Plain Turkish |
|----------------|---------------|
| deadline | son tarih |
| feedback | geri bildirim |
| workshop | calisma grubu |

---

## Formatting [M]

### A. Structure [M]
- Use headings and subheadings [M]
- Use bullet points for lists of 3+ items [C]
- Use numbered lists for sequential steps [M]

### B. Paragraphs [M]
- Keep paragraphs focused on one topic [M]
- Maximum 5-6 sentences per paragraph [C]
- Use white space effectively [C]

### C. Emphasis [C]
- Use **bold** for key terms on first mention [C]
- Avoid ALL CAPS for emphasis [M]
- Use italics sparingly [C]

---

## Numbers and Data [M]

### A. Numbers [M]
- Write 1-9 as words in running text (bir, iki, uc...) [C]
- Write 10+ as digits (10, 100, 1.000) [C]
- Use digits for all numbers in technical contexts [M]

### B. Dates [M]
- Full format: 15 Ocak 2024 [M]
- Abbreviated: 15.01.2024 [C]
- Be consistent within document [M]

### C. Percentages and Currency [M]
- Use: %25, 100 TL [M]
- Avoid: yuzde yirmi bes (in formal documents) [C]

---

## Special Cases [M]

### A. Abbreviations [M]
- Write out on first use with abbreviation in parentheses [M]
- Example: Turkiye Buyuk Millet Meclisi (TBMM)
- Common abbreviations (vb., vs.) may be used [C]

### B. Acronyms [M]
- Treat like abbreviations [M]
- Use consistently after first introduction [M]

### C. Legal and Official Terms [M]
- Preserve official terminology [M]
- Provide brief explanation if needed [M]
- Do not paraphrase legal definitions [M]
