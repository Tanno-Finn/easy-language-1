# Directive: Thai Plain Language (ภาษาไทยเข้าใจง่าย)

## Basis [M]
**Type:** Adapted Standard
**Framework:** Based on general Plain Language principles adapted for Thai
**Origin:** Thailand

---

## Purpose [M]
Defines rules for translating texts into Thai Plain Language - clear, accessible writing for the general public.
**Target Audience:** General readers with average literacy (B1-B2 level equivalent).

## Key Difference from Easy Thai [C]
Plain Thai is **less restrictive** than Easy Thai:
- Allows longer sentences when necessary
- Permits more formal vocabulary
- Focuses on clarity rather than maximum simplification

---

## Core Principles [M]

### The Hybrid Rule [M]
Technical terms **may be preserved** and explained when necessary for accuracy.

#### The Anchor Phrase [M]
Use the Thai phrase: **"หมายความว่า:"** or **"กล่าวคือ:"** (That is to say).

**Example:**
> **ดุลยภาพ**.
>
> กล่าวคือ:
>
> สภาวะที่สิ่งต่างๆ อยู่ในความสมดุล

---

## Thai-Specific Guidelines [M]

### Word Segmentation [M]
Thai has no spaces between words. For Plain Thai:
- Add spaces between clauses for readability
- Keep natural word groupings together
- Less aggressive segmentation than Easy Thai

**Example:**
> Standard: การประชุมคณะกรรมการจะจัดขึ้นในวันพุธหน้า
> Plain Thai: การประชุมคณะกรรมการ จะจัดขึ้นในวันพุธหน้า

### Sentence Structure [M]
- **Aim for one main idea per sentence** [M]
- **Maximum 25 words per sentence** (guideline) [C]
- **Prefer active voice**, passive acceptable when appropriate [M]
- **Maintain clear subject-verb relationships** [M]

### Vocabulary [M]
- Use standard Thai vocabulary
- Formal vocabulary acceptable when appropriate
- Explain technical terms on first use
- Avoid overly bureaucratic language (ภาษาราชการที่ซับซ้อน)

### Clarity Guidelines [M]
- Put important information first
- Use parallel structure in lists
- Break complex ideas into steps
- Use examples to illustrate abstract concepts

---

## Grammar Rules [M]

### Sentence Construction [M]
- Keep subject and verb close together
- Place modifiers near the words they modify
- Use connectors (และ, แต่, เพราะ, ดังนั้น) to show relationships
- Avoid ambiguous pronoun references

### Avoid [M]
- Unnecessarily complex sentences
- Bureaucratic jargon without explanation
- Excessive nominalizations
- Strings of prepositional phrases

### Acceptable [C]
- Compound sentences with clear connectors
- Standard formal vocabulary
- Passive voice when agent is unknown or unimportant
- Pali-Sanskrit terms when commonly understood

---

## Formatting [C]

### Structure
- Use headings to organize content
- Use bullet points for lists
- Keep paragraphs focused (3-5 sentences)
- Use bold for key terms

### Numbers & Dates
- Use Arabic numerals for clarity (1, 2, 3)
- Write out numbers under ten in text (optional)
- Full date format: 15 มกราคม พ.ศ. 2567
