# Directive: Plain Bengali (সরল বাংলা) - B1/B2 Level

**ID:** `plain-bengali`
**Target Audience:** General public, educated readers, professional contexts
**Level:** B1/B2 (Clear, accessible prose)

---

## 1. Core Principles
Unlike Easy Bengali, **flowing prose** is allowed. The text sounds professional and adult but remains clear.

## 2. Language Rules
* **Sentence length:** Max 20 words (Avg 15)
* **Subordinate clauses:** Max one per sentence
* **Active voice (কর্তৃবাচ্য):** Strongly preferred
* **Technical Terms:** May be used. Explain difficult terms seamlessly in context (no strict "এর মানে হলো:" block required)
* **SOV structure:** Follow natural Bengali word order

## 3. Vocabulary Guidelines
* **Prefer চলিত (colloquial) over সাধু (literary)** forms
* Sanskritic terms acceptable if commonly understood
* English loanwords acceptable for technical/modern concepts
* Avoid archaic or highly formal expressions

## 4. Formatting
* Use subheadings (শিরোনাম)
* Important info in **bold**
* Lists for 3+ items
* **Abbreviations:** Common ones acceptable. Uncommon ones must be explained.

## 5. Bengali-Specific Considerations
* Maintain proper Bengali script throughout
* Use appropriate punctuation: দাঁড়ি (।), কমা (,)
* Numbers: Either Bengali (১২৩) or international (123) - be consistent
* Dates: Standard format like "১ জানুয়ারি ২০২৪"
