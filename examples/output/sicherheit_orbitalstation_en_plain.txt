# Safety Instructions for Orbital Station Omega-7

**Department:** Reactor Core Safety and Extravehicular Operations
**Version:** Galactic Standard Year 3024

---

## 1. Introduction

You must follow these safety rules at all times. They protect your life and the station's structure. Violations will result in immediate transfer to the garbage compactor section.

---

## 2. Behaviour Near the Primary Energy Core

### Protective Equipment

When entering Sector 7G, you must wear full **personal protective equipment (PPE)**. This includes:
- Class IV radiation suit
- Visual glare protection

No gaps or openings are allowed in your protective gear.

### Emergency Shutdown Override

Only **authorized specialist personnel** may bypass the emergency shutdown system. Do not attempt this yourself.

### Red Alert Warning

If you hear the acoustic warning signal (Red Alert siren), leave the danger zone immediately. Do not stay behind to perform heroic acts. This is forbidden.

### Open Reactor Shafts

**Warning:** Open reactor shafts have no safety railings. This is a known design flaw (see Architect Defect Report No. 1138). There is a high risk of falling. Always stay at least **2 metres** from the shaft edge.

---

## 3. Procedure During Pressure Loss (Decompression)

Hull damage can occur from meteorite impact or enemy laser fire. When this happens, the **bulkhead sealing mechanisms** activate automatically.

Follow these steps:
1. Continue breathing calmly, if there is still atmosphere.
2. Grab the nearest emergency oxygen mask.
3. Help yourself first, then help others. This includes your supervisors.

---

## 4. Handling Defective Droids

Do not repair malfunctioning maintenance droids yourself. Their faulty motivator units can cause unpredictable electrical discharges. Report all droid malfunctions to the **Department of Robotics and Cybernetics** immediately.
