1. Introduction to Work Safety

You must follow these safety rules.
The rules protect your life.
The rules also protect the station.

2. How to Behave Near the Main Energy Core

Section a:
You must wear **PPE** when you enter Sector 7G.

That means:

Personal Protective Equipment.

Your PPE includes 2 items.

Item 1: A radiation suit (Class 4).

Item 2: Eye protection against bright light.

You must wear all items.
No gaps allowed.

Section b:
Only special staff can turn off the **emergency shut-down device**.

That means:

The system that stops the reactor in danger.

You need permission to touch it.

Section c:
You may hear a loud alarm.
This alarm is called "Red Alert".
You must leave the danger area right away.

Do not stay to be a hero.
That is not allowed.

Section d:
There are open reactor shafts.
These shafts have no railings.
This is a design problem.
See report number 1138.

You could fall into the shaft.
Stay at least 2 metres away from the edge.

3. What to Do When Air Pressure Drops

This is called **decompression**.

That means:

Air escapes from the station.

This can happen for 2 reasons.

Reason 1: A meteor hits the station.

Reason 2: Enemy lasers damage the hull.

The station has **bulkhead locks**.

That means:

Big doors that close to seal off sections.

These doors close by themselves.

Follow these 3 steps:

Step a:
Keep breathing calmly.
Only if there is still air.

Step b:
Grab the nearest emergency oxygen mask.

Step c:
Help yourself first.
Then help others.
This includes your boss.

---

4. How to Handle Broken Droids

**Droids** are robots that do maintenance work.

Sometimes droids stop working.
Do not try to fix them yourself.

Some droids have broken **motivator units**.

That means:

The part that makes them move.

Broken motivators can cause electric shocks.
These shocks are dangerous.

Report all broken droids right away.
Tell the Department of Robotics and Cybernetics.
